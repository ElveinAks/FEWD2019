Purpose

The goal of this site is for me to not only practice front-end development, but also to make a reference for myself. This reference will be used in the future, both for information and motivation for learning languages. Methodologies and resources will be updated as needed, and new features will be added to the site to help make it more productive; this has the added benefit of maintaining my web-development skills, along with practicing new skills.

Why Learn?

There are reasonable answers to the inquiry of why I should learn languages, with the current answers to this question only being to the benefit of the spirit of language acquisition. The practicality of the matter cannot be overstated, nor can it be removed from the core of the majority of the reasons to be mentioned. To start, it will give me a better understanding of intention (whether by speaker or author), which is ultimately what language comprehension can be summed up by. Second, it leads to a better understanding of the human thought process, which is applicable in many fields. Learning languages can lead to more job opportunities, more interesting jobs and better paid jobs. One worthy mention is self-satisfaction, the importance of which is outside of the scope of this project.

Methods & Resources

Books

These are both free and paid resources for learning languages. Hard copies are better, PDF's are bearable and online books are discouraged: This has been my pattern. So far, I've only had experience with Teach Yourself, but I have heard great things about Colloquial. Don't forget to incorporate reading into your regiment, usually done after completing one or two books in the target language. The online books have some bizarre languages.
